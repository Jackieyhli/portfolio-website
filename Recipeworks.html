Recipe Works

Overview

Design a responsive web app for recipes that meets the needs of its users and solves the problems they are facing with existing recipe apps.

Problem

Many recipe sites on the market offer long overcomplicated cooking instructions that drive users away.

Solution

Designing an application that has simple and straight to the point instructions paired with high quality photos and video.

Process

Research - Competitive Analysis

Kitchen Stories

SWOT Analysis

Strengths:

  • Simple and easy to use interface

Weaknesses:

  • Possibly their lack of instructional cook videos with every meal

Opportunities:

  • Make short cooking videos to pair along with the step by step photos for every recipe.

Threats:

  • Tik Tok and apps like Tasty that utilize fast short cut videos demonstrating the cooking.

UX Analysis

Usability: Easy to navigate through and find information via the interface.

Layout: Clean and visually pleasing with nice spacing in-between elements Navigation structure: Easy to navigate through and remember.

Compatibility: Kitchen stories currently supports iPhone, iPad and Android.

Calls to Action: Users are free to browse the Kitchen Stories app without any annoying notifications pushing them to sign up for anything. However when scrolling through the home page they have the option to purchase a premium plus version as well as an amazon cook book from them.

Interviews

I conducted interviews to get a better sense of potential users of my future app using the following questions.

1. what is your relationship like with food? (eg.,I watch my diet and eat healthy, I binge eat fast food)

2. How much time do you typically spend cooking?

3. What do you feel is the hardest part about cooking?

4. What is your level/expertise in cooking?

5. If you are currently using a cooking app/website tell more about your experience using it

6. What features would you find to be the most important in a cooking app?

Conclusions from Interview:

Finding the time to cook seemed to be a common factor in all three participates answers pertaining to the hardest part of cooking with all three participants preferring to only spend 30 minutes on it. The participants sought video, clear pictures and simple and short cooking directions in in a recipe app.

User Persona

Defining the user and their needs

Who is the user?

  1. Beginners who are just getting into cooking
  2. Food lovers or “foodies” who want to try out new and tasty recipes
  3. Busy on the go people who need quick and easy meals

What major goals and tasks will users want/need to accomplish?

  • Create profile
  • Filter food recipes by dietary restriction or other needs
  • Bookmark and save recipes
  • Add the ingredients of the recipe to a shopping list
  • Find recipes

What will the product do?

Offer new recipes that can be saved and shared among social media platforms to friends and family. As well users new to cooking may watch videos and learn the fundamentals of cooking such as cutting techniques, how to slice an onion, etc.

Why does the user need the product?

To find new and tasty recipes and learn cooking techniques.

What kind of information will be created, entered , or featured in the product?

User profiles, dietary restrictions, recipes, ingredient lists and serving sizes.

Are there any existing development, design, or business rules that need to be followed?

Include Nutrition per serving, time, difficulty level, steps and ingredients needed.

MVP: Create a responsive recipe website that will offer recipes with video and pictures that can be filtered by dietary restrictions or preference, as well be able to add the needed ingredients to a shopping list.
